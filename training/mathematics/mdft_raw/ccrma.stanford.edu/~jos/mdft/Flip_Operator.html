Flip Operator

We define the flip operator by

\begin{equation}\oper{Flip}_n(x) \isdef x(-n), \end{equation}

for all sample indices \(n\in\mathbb{Z}\). By modulo indexing, \(x(-n)\) is the same as \(x(N-n)\). The \(\oper{Flip}()\) operator reverses the order of samples \(1\) through \(N-1\) of a sequence, leaving sample \(0\) alone, as shown in Fig.7.1a. Thanks to modulo indexing, it can also be viewed as ``flipping'' the sequence about the time 0, as shown in Fig.7.1b. The interpretation of Fig.7.1b is usually the one we want, and the \(\oper{Flip}\) operator is usually thought of as ``time reversal'' when applied to a signal \(x\) or ``frequency reversal'' when applied to a spectrum \(X\).
