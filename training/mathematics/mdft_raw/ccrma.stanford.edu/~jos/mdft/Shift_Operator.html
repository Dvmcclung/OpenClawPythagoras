Shift Operator

The shift operator is defined by

\[\oper{Shift}_{\Delta,n}(x) \isdef x(n-\Delta), \quad \Delta\in\mathbb{Z},\]

and \(\oper{Shift}_{\Delta}(x)\) denotes the entire shifted signal. Note that since indexing is modulo \(N\), the shift is circular (or ``cyclic''). However, we normally use it to represent time delay by \(\Delta\) samples. We often use the shift operator in conjunction with zero padding (appending zeros to the signal \(x\), §7.2.7) in order to avoid the ``wrap-around'' associated with a circular shift.
