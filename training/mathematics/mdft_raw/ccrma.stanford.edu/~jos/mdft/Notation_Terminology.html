Notation and Terminology

If \(X\) is the DFT of \(x\), we say that \(x\) and \(X\) form a transform pair and write

\[\zbox{x\;\longleftrightarrow\;X} \quad \mbox{(``$x$\ corresponds to $X$'')}.\]

Another notation we'll use is

\begin{eqnarray*}\oper{DFT}(x)&\isdef& X,\quad\mbox{and} \\ \oper{DFT}_k(x)&\isdef& X(k).\end{eqnarray*}

If we need to indicate the length of the DFT explicitly, we will write \(\oper{DFT}_N(x) = X\) and \(\oper{DFT}_{N,k}(x) = X(k)\). As we've already seen, time-domain signals are consistently denoted using lowercase symbols such as ``\(x(n)\),'' while frequency-domain signals (spectra), are denoted in uppercase (``\(X(\omega_k)\)'').
