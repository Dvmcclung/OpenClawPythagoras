Negative Exponents

What should \(a^{-1}\) be? Multiplying it by \(a\) gives, using property (1),

\[a^{-1} \cdot a = a^{-1} a^1 = a^{-1+1} = a^0 = 1.\]

Dividing through by \(a\) then gives

\[\zbox{a^{-1} = \frac{1}{a}.}\]

Similarly, we obtain

\[\zbox{a^{-M} = \frac{1}{a^M}}\]

for all integer values of \(M\), i.e., \(\forall M\in\mathbb{Z}\).
